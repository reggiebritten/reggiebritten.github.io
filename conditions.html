Conditions

Conditions are descriptions of a temporary situation a character finds themselves in. Most are bad, some are good, and others may be a mix of both depending on the circumstances. Many conditions are inflicted during combat, but some arise outside combat as well. Each condition lasts until something happens to end it and applies a specific set of bonuses or penalties to the affected character.

Just like abilities, conditions have tags that describe how they interact with other things. The most important tag a condition might have is the recoverable tag, which means that the recovery ability may be used to attempt to end the condition.

However, all recoverable conditions come with recovery requirements that must be met for the recovery ability to be successful. Some conditions require vigor loss, which may be paid either by the character using the recovery ability, the character affected by the condition, or a combination of both. Other conditions have other requirements that must be met as well.

Some effects may also allow characters to directly remove or replace conditions while ignoring any recovery requirements.

Condition Tags

Just like abilities, conditions also have tags that describe how they work and interact with other parts of the game.

Conferred Reistance, Vulnerability & Immunity

Some conditions change the way the character is affected by certain abilities

Disabled-ZoC

A condition with the Disabled-ZoC tag means the character suffering from it does not have a zone of control, no matter what weapons they wield.

Disabled-Reactions

A condition with the Disabled -Reactions tag prevents the character suffering from it from using any reaction abilities.

Disabled-Swapping

A condition with the Disabled-Swapping tag prevents the character suffering from it from using swapping weapons under any circumstances.

Many conditions have a removed by clause that specifies something that will end the condition when it happens, or a replaced by clause

Recovery: Either the restrained character or the character using the recovery ability must succeed on a brawn roll to break the restraints, with a TN determined by the material of their restraints. If the roll fails, the character is still restrained.

Grabbed

üè∑Ô∏è Physical No-ZoC

A grabbed character is being held in place by one or more other characters.

Characters involved in the grab lose their zones of control and suffer a bane on attack actions against characters who are not part of the grab.

If the grabbing character and the gabbed character are within one size category of each other, then the grabbed character cannot move or use movement abilities. The grabbing character can drag the grabbed character along whenever they move, but they move half the usual distance. If two similar sized characters grab each other, then neither can move.

If the characters are two or more size category apart, then the larger character may move freely and the smaller character is pulled along, regardless of who is grabbing whom.

If three or more characters are involved in the grab, then none of them can move unless they are two or more sizes bigger than every other character.

Whenever any character in the grab would be pushed, the grabbing character must choose: either they must lose -3 momentum, and all characters are pushed, or the grab ends and only the original target of the effect is pushed.

The grabbing character almost always needs to concentrate to maintain the grabbed condition. Interrupting the grabbing character is often the best way to free a grabbed character.

Hidden

üè∑Ô∏è Circumstantial

A hidden character has eluded the perception of everyone looking for them. A character who wants to become hidden should consider the hide maneuver. If a character begins their turn hidden or becomes hidden on their turn, they retain the element of surprise and remain hidden until the end of their turn or until they use an action.

A hidden character adds a boon to their attack actions, however almost all actions or attacks will cause the character to no longer be hidden at the end of the turn.

While the character remains hidden, enemies cannot target the character with attacks except area attacks, and the character may ignore enemy zones of control. If a character is obviously noticed or makes a significant amount of noise, like an audible ability, the hidden condition is removed from them.
