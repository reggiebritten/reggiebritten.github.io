Combat in Water

Fighting while swimming can be extremely difficult if you are unprepared. Spaces of water count as wet-terrain, and require an extra point of movement for every space moved. You automatically become wet while swimming, and the wet condition is instantly reapplied if it is ever removed. All your skills suffer a bane while you are swimming, unless they are made with a Dagger, Short Spear, or similar weapon. The GM may further rule that some skills are impossible or have other penalties underwater.

If you are unexpectedly plunged under water, you suffer the choking condition, and that condition is persistent while you remain under water. However, if you intentionally hold your breath on your turn, you are immune to the choking condition, but you lose -2 momentum and -2 focus at the end of your turn. See the breath holding rules for dives lasting longer than a few rounds.
