Skills

Skills are specific things a character can do to help achieve their goals. Some skills have useful effects outside of combat, while others help characters defeat their enemies and protect their allies. All skills have a type, a target, and tags, that describe how the skill may be used. Most skills have effects that describe what the skill does when used, and some skills may also cost or grant defense when used.

Skill Types

Each skill has a type that describes how it is used in combat. There are four types of skills: Actions, Maneuvers, Reactions, & Passives.

Actions

Actions are often a character’s most powerful skills, including most attacks against enemies and skills to support allies. Characters may use one action on their turn. Actions may benefit from a character’s agility, brawn, and/or cunning, depending on the action’s target, as detailed in the skill targets section below. Actions require a challenge to see how well they succeed, as detailed in the action challenge section below. After a character uses an action, or replaced their action with a maneuver, all temporary conditions are removed from the character.

Maneuvers

Each character may only use one maneuver each turn. A character can give up their action to use a second maneuver on their turn, but if they do, they become flat-footed. A character cannot use an action in the middle of a maneuver, or vice-versa. A character may move up to their speed as part of any maneuver that isn’t slow, but not maneuvers that are slow. Using a maneuver in an enemy’s zone of control risks being hinder, unless it it careful. A Maneuver doesn’t normally involve a challenge unless it involves moving though an unusual area.

Reactions

Reactions may be used at any time, even during another character’s turn and interrupting another a skill. However, all reactions have a trigger, which must be met before the reaction can be used. A character can use reactions as often as their triggers are met are met, but normally only once per triggering event. A reaction doesn’t normally involve a challenge.

Passive

Passive skills are always in effect, and a character always receives their benefits.

Other

Other skills take more than one action, and will specify how long they take to complete. A character must concentrate on the skill for the duration in order to complete the skill. If they are interrupted, then nothing happens, and any resources spent on the skill are not consumed.

Skill Targets

All non-passive skills have a target, which describes who or what is affected by the skill. A character must be able to see the target(s) of a skill, with the exception of burst skills and skills that only target the user. The targets of a skill must be chosen before any effects, costs, threat or yields come into effect. If a skill targets multiple different characters, then each may independently choose how to respond to the skill. Some skills may also specify special targeting rules that must be followed.

Utility

Utility skills are the broadest category of skills, and may aid oneself or allies in or outside of combat. Unless specified otherwise, a character can target themselves with utility skills, and the user us always considered the target of a utility skill that is neither melee or ranged. Utility skills are capable of affecting enemies as well, but they are never attacks. Whenever a character uses a utility action, they may choose one of two bonuses to add to the effect. Either they themselves gain momentum equal to their brawn, or one target gains focus equal to their cunning. If the character targets themselves, they may gain either momentum or focus.

Attack

An attack skill lets you wear down your target’s defenses and ultimately defeat them. In addition to the usual features of a skill, all attacks have a threat and a yield. The target of your attack most choose between the threat or the yield, both of which will be harmful in different ways. See the attacking & defending section for details on what to do when you are attacked.

Threat

The threat of the attack represents how difficult an attack is to avoid. Each attack will tell you it’s base threat, and you add your agility to that threat when you use that attack. You must take an attack’s threat as your choice of poise, momentum, or focus damage if you want to avoid that attack’s yield. This is detailed in the attacking & defending section.

Yield

An attack’s yield describes what the attack does if it is not defended against. Each attack has a damage type (poise, momentum, or focus) and damage dice that you roll if the target yields. Add together all the dice to determine the amount of damage you do with the attack. If it is a melee attack, also add your brawn to this damage. The target’s armor reduces the damage they take from an attack’s yield.

Cunning Effects

For each of your skill’s dice that roll below your cunning, you have earned a cunning effect! Unlike an attack’s damage, a skill’s dice are not added together to determine cunning effects. Instead, each die is considered individually, and multiple dice below your cunning can earn multiple cunning effects. The type of cunning effects you may add to the skill depend on the skill’s tags, and will usually offer you a choice. For example, a water cunning effect can make the target wet or remove a condition from them. If your skill earns three or more cunning effects, then it becomes fated instead! Describe to the GM any effect you can imagine your skill having, and the GM will tell you if it is reasonable, and what happens.

Melee

A melee skill can target one character who is within one space of the character using the skill. A melee utility skill can usually target the character using it, but melee attacks can’t. A character adds their brawn to the damage of melee attack actions.

Ranged

A ranged skill will specify a maximum range, such as “Ranged: 10”. This range is increased by a character’s cunning. A ranged skill can target one character within the maximum range. Using a ranged action in an enemy’s zone of control let’s that enemy impose a bane on the skill.

Line

A line always specifies a range and begins from the character using the skill. The character may draw a line in any direction, and the skill targets any space or character that the line passes though, up to the maximum range. Line skills require precision and cannot target a character that cannot be seen; they automatically fail to affect unseen characters.

Burst

A burst is a sphere that spreads out from the center a number of spaces specified by the skill. For example, a two-space burst would include the center of the area as well as all spaces up to two spaces away from the center. Bursts cannot normally go through walls, but can spread around them. A burst can be central, point-blank, or within range. A central burst means the area of the skill must be directly centered on the character using it If a character occupies more than one space, they choose one of their spaces as the center of the burst. A point-blank burst means that at least one space of the burst must be adjacent to the character using the skill, and no space can include the character. A burst within range means that the center of the burst may be anywhere within the range of the skill. Note that the range applies to the center of the area, so it is possible for a character to be affected even beyond the listed range.

Skill Costs

A skill may list a type of defense that it grants or costs when used. A positive number grants the character using it the specified amount of the specified type of defense. If the character can’t currently gain that type of defense, for example if they are knocked-down and trying to gain momentum, then the character may still use the skill but they gain no defense from it. A negative number means that the skill costs defense, which must be paid before the skill can be used; this cost is unaffected by a character’s armor. If the character cannot pay the cost, they cannot use the skill. If a reaction changes the character’s defense after they use the skill but before it comes into effect and leaves them unable to pay the cost, then the skill does nothing and the action, maneuver, or reaction opportunity used on the skill is wasted. A character may invoke a final gambit to use a skill without paying its defense cost, but doing so leaves the character drained of energy and automatically defeated.

Skill Effects

A skill’s effects describe what it actually does when used. Most skills have effects, except for some straightforward attacks. Effects of a skill are not usually optional, they happen weather or not a character wants them to happen, although they may give a character options. Effects of a skill are always decided and resolved after the cost of the skill and before any other rules. If there is ever any uncertainty regarding exactly what a skill’s effects do, the GM will make the final decision.

Concentration

Some skills require the user’s active concentration to maintain the effects. Unlike most other effects of a skill, concentration effects may be ignored by the character using the skill if desired. While a character is concentrating on an effect, they may release their concentration any time on a friendly character’s turn, but not on an enemy’s turn. Concentrating on a skill doesn’t come with any inherent cost or penalty, however it does have a few stipulations. For one, a character may only concentrate on one skill at a time. If a character uses a second concentration skill while they are already concentrating on another one, then they must either release concentration on the first skill or ignore the concentration effects of the second skill.

Interrupting Concentration

A character who is concentrating can be interrupted by other skills and effects. Interrupting a character is also an option for an attack’s twist of fate (but not a critical). When a character’s concentration is interrupted, any effects they were concentrating on immediately end. Some concentration effects are automatically interrupted when specific events come to pass, and some skills might do something special when they are interrupted.

Action Challenges

A challenge is required every time a character uses a utility action skill, or yields to an attack action skill. Challenges aren’t normally used for maneuvers, reactions, or passive skills. The target number for an action challenge is 10, and the character using the skill further reduces the TN by their cunning. Other effects, such as boons and banes, can also modify a skill’s TN. Two ten-sided dice are rolled, however, the results of an action challenge are interpreted differently than a normal challenge. If none of the dice beat the TN, then nothing happens; no additional complications arise. If exactly one of the dice beat the TN, then the action is a cunning-effect! Critical actions grant bonus effects based on the type of skill used. If two or more dice beat the TN, then the action is fated! The character may choose two critical effects or invoke a free twist of fate related to the action. However, If all dice involved in an action challenge roll “1” or below, then the action is doomed and suffers a complication. If an action has multiple targets, a separate challenge should be rolled for each.

Cunning Effects

For each of your skill’s dice that roll below your cunning, you have earned a cunning effect! Unlike an attack’s damage, a skill’s dice are not added together to determine cunning effects. Instead, each die is considered individually, and multiple dice below your cunning can earn multiple cunning effects. The type of cunning effects you may add to the skill depend on the skill’s tags, and will usually offer you a choice. For example, a water cunning effect can make the target wet or remove a condition from them. If your skill earns three or more cunning effects, then it becomes fated instead! Describe to the GM any effect you can imagine your skill having, and the GM will tell you if it is reasonable, and what happens.

Boons

Each boon that your action receives lets you roll one additional die for the action, and increases your action’s threat by +4 if it is an attack.

Banes

Each bane that your action receives cancels out one boon that the action has. Each excess bane removed one of the action’s dice, and reduces the threat by -4 if it is an attack. An action’s dice can be reduced to 0, but not below 0. Even if an action has 0 dice, any other effects still take place.

Skill Tags

A skill’s tags may describe many things about the skill, included where it comes from, how it may be used, and what it does when used. For example, skills with the weapon tag come from weapons, armor skill come from armor, legendary skills are associated with a personal legend or legendary character. A skill’s tags also specify which class(es) may learn the skill, and weather the skill is a core feature of the class or requires a certain tier to unlock. A complete glossary of skill tags can be found below, and players should refer back to this list when necessary.

Careful

A careful maneuver cannot be hinder, and a careful ranged action does not suffer a bane when it is used in an enemy zone of control.

Slow

A slow maneuver doesn’t let a character move their bonus speed as part of the skill. Slow maneuvers may still let the character move a specific number of spaces.

Fire

A fire skill wields the elemental power of flame. Cunning: The target becomes burning or blinded.

Sight

A sight skill relies on bright light or visual patterns for it’s effect. A light skill is ineffective on a blinded character, and a blinded character may be unable to use certain light skills at the GM’s discretion. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes blinded or guarded.

Air

An air skill wields the elemental power of the winds. Cunning: the target becomes vortexed, choking, or evasive.

Barehanded

A barehanded skill requires the use of one free hand, and typically can’t be used while wielding a separate weapon in each hand. A barehanded skill may be used while wielding one two-handed weapon, but you cannot use a barehanded skill white concentrating on a two-handed weapon’s skill and vice versa. Cunning: The target becomes grabbed while you concentrate, you take one of the target’s weapons, or you remove a non-persistent condition from the target.

Slashing

  A slashing skill slices with a keen edge.   Cunning: The target becomes bleeding or deal +3 poise damage.

Piercing

  A piercing skill stabs with an impaling point.   Cunning: This skill ignores the target’s armor or deals +3 focus damage.

Blunt

A blunt skill smashes with brute force. A punch, hammer strike, or hurled boulder would all be blunt. Cunning: Shove the target three spaces or deal +3 momentum damage.

Lightning

A lightning skill wields the elemental power of electricity. Cunning: The target becomes shocked or disarmed.

Ice

A ice skill wields the elemental power of frost. Cunning: The target becomes chilled or weakened.

Water

A water skill wields the elemental power of water. Cunning: The target becomes wet, or you remove a non-persistent condition from the target.

Earth

An earth skill wields the elemental power of stone. Cunning: The target becomes petrified, guarded, or staggered.

Caustic

A caustic skill wields the elemental power of acid. Cunning: The target becomes corroding or wracked.

Oil

An oil skill wields the elemental power of grease. Cunning: The target becomes oiled, evasive, or fumbling.

Holy

A holy skill wields the righteous power of the divine. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes blessed or guarded.

Unholy

An unholy skill wields the wicked power of the dammed. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes cursed or defenseless.

Plant

A plant skill wields the natural power of flora. Cunning: The target becomes retrained or guarded.

Mental

A mental skill manipulates a character’s mind. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes distracted or honed.

Sound

A sound skill relies on shouting, music, or other noise for it’s effect. A sound skill is ineffective on a deafened character, and a deafened or choking character may be unable to use certain sound skills at the GM’s discretion. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes deafened or empowered.

Weapon

A weapon skill requires the user to wield the skill’s weapon in the proper way in order to use it. Two handed weapons must be wielded in both hands to use any of their skills. If a character is not proficient in a weapon, then they can only use the first half, rounded down, of that weapon’s skills.

Unblockable

The target of an unblockable attack can’t use momentum to defend against the attack. They must either dodge, predict, or yield the attack.

Undodgeable

The target of an undodgeable attack can’t use poise to defend against the attack. They must either block, predict, or yield the attack.

Unpredictable

The target of an unpredictable attack can’t use focus to defend against the attack. They must either block, dodge, or yield the attack.

Universal

A universal skill is available to any character at any time, regardless of equipped weapons or fighting styles. Characters may still be prevented from using universal skills by outside forces, such aw the restrained condition preventing a character from dashing. The GM may rule that some universal skills are unavailable to certain characters, for example, a snake cannot reasonably use the kick skill.

Legendary

A legendary skill is unique to a legendary foe or a character’s personal legend.

Core

A core skill is the defining feature of a class, and is automatically included as a part of that classes fighting style. Core skills don’t count against the number of skills a character can equip to their fighting style.

Basic

A basic skill is available to every member of a specific class, and does not need to be unlocked with experience.

Stealthy

A stealthy skill is relatively inconspicuous, and does not automatically remove the hidden condition from the character using it. However, the GM will usually call for an agility roll to remain hidden even when using a stealthy skill.

Vulnerable

If a character is vulnerable to a specific tag, then any attack action with that tag that targets them gains a boon. utility skills and attacks that aren’t actions are unaffected by vulnerability. If a character is vulnerable to multiple tags, then each will add a boon to attacks targeting them. However, multiple sources of vulnerability to the same tag don’t add multiple boons.

Resistant

If a character is resistant to a specific tag, then any attack action with that tag that targets them suffers a bane. utility skills and attacks that aren’t actions are unaffected by resistance. If a character is resistant to multiple tags, then each will add a bane to attacks targeting them. However, multiple sources of resistance to the same tag don’t add multiple banes.

Immune

If a character is immune to a condition, then that condition can’t be applied to that character. If a character is immune to a specific tag, then the threat of any any attack with that tag is reduced to 0, effectively allowing the character to ignore that attack. In addition, a character who is immune to an attack’s tag may choose to automatically make that attack doomed. Note that a character only needs to be immune to one of a skill’s tags, not all of them.

Ineffective

If a skill with a particular tag is ineffective on a character, then that character can’t be affected by the skill in any way, whether they want to be affected or not.
