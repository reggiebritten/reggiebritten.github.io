Skill Tags

A skill’s tags may describe many things about the skill, included where it comes from, how it may be used, and what it does when used. For example, skills with the weapon tag come from weapons, armor skill come from armor, legendary skills are associated with a personal legend or legendary character. A skill’s tags also specify which class(es) may learn the skill, and weather the skill is a core feature of the class or requires a certain tier to unlock. A complete glossary of skill tags can be found below, and players should refer back to this list when necessary.

Careful

A careful maneuver cannot be hinder, and a careful ranged action does not suffer a bane when it is used in an enemy zone of control.

Slow

A slow maneuver doesn’t let a character move their bonus speed as part of the skill. Slow maneuvers may still let the character move a specific number of spaces.

Fire

A fire skill wields the elemental power of flame. Cunning: The target becomes burning or blinded.

Sight

A sight skill relies on bright light or visual patterns for it’s effect. A light skill is ineffective on a blinded character, and a blinded character may be unable to use certain light skills at the GM’s discretion. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes blinded or guarded.

Air

An air skill wields the elemental power of the winds. Cunning: the target becomes vortexed, choking, or evasive.

Barehanded

A barehanded skill requires the use of one free hand, and typically can’t be used while wielding a separate weapon in each hand. A barehanded skill may be used while wielding one two-handed weapon, but you cannot use a barehanded skill white concentrating on a two-handed weapon’s skill and vice versa. Cunning: The target becomes grabbed while you concentrate, you take one of the target’s weapons, or you remove a non-persistent condition from the target.

Slashing

  A slashing skill slices with a keen edge.   Cunning: The target becomes bleeding or deal +3 poise damage.

Piercing

  A piercing skill stabs with an impaling point.   Cunning: This skill ignores the target’s armor or deals +3 focus damage.

Blunt

A blunt skill smashes with brute force. A punch, hammer strike, or hurled boulder would all be blunt. Cunning: Shove the target three spaces or deal +3 momentum damage.

Lightning

A lightning skill wields the elemental power of electricity. Cunning: The target becomes shocked or disarmed.

Ice

A ice skill wields the elemental power of frost. Cunning: The target becomes chilled or weakened.

Water

A water skill wields the elemental power of water. Cunning: The target becomes wet, or you remove a non-persistent condition from the target.

Earth

An earth skill wields the elemental power of stone. Cunning: The target becomes petrified, guarded, or staggered.

Caustic

A caustic skill wields the elemental power of acid. Cunning: The target becomes corroding or wracked.

Oil

An oil skill wields the elemental power of grease. Cunning: The target becomes oiled, evasive, or fumbling.

Holy

A holy skill wields the righteous power of the divine. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes blessed or guarded.

Unholy

An unholy skill wields the wicked power of the dammed. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes cursed or defenseless.

Plant

A plant skill wields the natural power of flora. Cunning: The target becomes retrained or guarded.

Mental

A mental skill manipulates a character’s mind. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes distracted or honed.

Sound

A sound skill relies on shouting, music, or other noise for it’s effect. A sound skill is ineffective on a deafened character, and a deafened or choking character may be unable to use certain sound skills at the GM’s discretion. Cunning: Either you or the target becomes deafened or empowered.

Weapon

A weapon skill requires the user to wield the skill’s weapon in the proper way in order to use it. Two handed weapons must be wielded in both hands to use any of their skills. If a character is not proficient in a weapon, then they can only use the first half, rounded down, of that weapon’s skills.

Unblockable

The target of an unblockable attack can’t use momentum to defend against the attack. They must either dodge, predict, or yield the attack.

Undodgeable

The target of an undodgeable attack can’t use poise to defend against the attack. They must either block, predict, or yield the attack.

Unpredictable

The target of an unpredictable attack can’t use focus to defend against the attack. They must either block, dodge, or yield the attack.

Universal

A universal skill is available to any character at any time, regardless of equipped weapons or fighting styles. Characters may still be prevented from using universal skills by outside forces, such aw the restrained condition preventing a character from dashing. The GM may rule that some universal skills are unavailable to certain characters, for example, a snake cannot reasonably use the kick skill.

Legendary

A legendary skill is unique to a legendary foe or a character’s personal legend.

Core

A core skill is the defining feature of a class, and is automatically included as a part of that classes fighting style. Core skills don’t count against the number of skills a character can equip to their fighting style.

Basic

A basic skill is available to every member of a specific class, and does not need to be unlocked with experience.

Stealthy

A stealthy skill is relatively inconspicuous, and does not automatically remove the hidden condition from the character using it. However, the GM will usually call for an agility roll to remain hidden even when using a stealthy skill.

Vulnerable

If a character is vulnerable to a specific tag, then any attack action with that tag that targets them gains a boon. utility skills and attacks that aren’t actions are unaffected by vulnerability. If a character is vulnerable to multiple tags, then each will add a boon to attacks targeting them. However, multiple sources of vulnerability to the same tag don’t add multiple boons.

Resistant

If a character is resistant to a specific tag, then any attack action with that tag that targets them suffers a bane. utility skills and attacks that aren’t actions are unaffected by resistance. If a character is resistant to multiple tags, then each will add a bane to attacks targeting them. However, multiple sources of resistance to the same tag don’t add multiple banes.

Immune

If a character is immune to a condition, then that condition can’t be applied to that character. If a character is immune to a specific tag, then the threat of any any attack with that tag is reduced to 0, effectively allowing the character to ignore that attack. In addition, a character who is immune to an attack’s tag may choose to automatically make that attack doomed. Note that a character only needs to be immune to one of a skill’s tags, not all of them.

Ineffective

If a skill with a particular tag is ineffective on a character, then that character can’t be affected by the skill in any way, whether they want to be affected or not.
