Special Terrain

Sometimes, the battlefield itself plays as big a role as the combatants. Special terrain is used to describe aspects of the area that have unique effects, especially during combat. Special terrain is often removed or replaced by other special terrain when certain conditions are met, but it is also possible for a space to have multiple special terrain effects.

Shattered Terrain

Shattered terrain is covered in large rocks or rubble. A space of shattered terrain requires two movement points to enter. A character knocked-down or kneeling in shattered terrain becomes unbalanced. If a floor becomes shattered terrain, it may collapse into an area below at the GM’s discretion. Wooden floors will usually collapse, but stone ones will usually not. Shattered terrain lasts indefinitely.

Overgrown Terrain

Overgrown terrain is covered in thick bushes, webs, or other fibers. A space of overgrown terrain requires two movement points to enter. A character in overgrown terrain adds a bane to all their attacks and all attacks against them. A character kneeling or knocked-down becomes restrained by the thick fibers. Overgrown terrain shrivels after 1 minute if it is not on fertile soil, otherwise it lasts indefinitely. A space of overgrown terrain becomes burning terrain if it is in a fire area or line ability, or if it contains a burning character.

Burning Terrain

Burning terrain is on fire. When a character enters burning terrain for the first time in a round or ends their turn there, they must lose -4 poise or they become burning. A character kneeling or knocked-down in burning terrain automatically becomes burning. If a space of burning terrain overlaps with a space of wet terrain, both are replaced by an obscuring cloud. If a space of burning terrain overlaps with a space of frozen terrain, both are replaced by wet terrain. Burning terrain in the area of an ice or water line or area effect is removed. Burning terrain returns to normal after 1 round unless it contains flammable material. If there is flammable material, then it lasts indefinitely and may spread to other flammable objects.

Wet Terrain

Wet terrain is covered in at least a foot of water (deep water is also wet terrain). A lightning area or line ability that includes a space of wet terrain also includes all adjacent spaces of wet terrain. A character knocked-down or keeling in wet terrain gains the wet condition. If a space of wet terrain overlaps with a space of burning terrain, then both are replaced by an obscuring cloud. Wet terrain is replaced by frozen terrain and becomes frozen terrain if it is in the area of an ice area or line ability. Wet terrain returns to normal at the end of the round if the water can flow away, otherwise it lasts indefinitely.

Frozen Terrain

Frozen terrain is covered in a thick sheet of ice or snow. When a character enters frozen terrain for the first time on a round or ends their turn there, they must succeed on a moderate agility roll or they become unbalanced. A character in frozen terrain is vulnerable to push abilities. A character kneeling or knocked-down in overgrown terrain becomes chilled. If a space of frozen terrain overlaps with a space of burning terrain, then both are replaced by wet terrain. Frozen terrain is removed if it is included in a fire area or line ability. Frozen terrain thaws after at least a minute, depending on the weather.

Goopy Terrain

Goopy terrain is covered in mud, sludge, or some other goo that makes moving and getting up difficult. A space of goopy terrain requires two movement points to enter. The kneeling condition can’t be removed from a creature in goopy terrain.

Oiled Terrain

Oiled terrain is covered in a layer of slippery, flammable oil. When a character enters oiled terrain for the first time on a round or ends their turn there, they must succeed on a moderate agility roll or they become unbalanced. A character in oiled terrain is vulnerable to push abilities. A character kneeling or knocked-down in oiled terrain becomes oiled. Oiled terrain is replaced by burning terrain and becomes burning terrain if it is in the area of a fire area or line ability or if it contains a burning character. If one space of oiled terrain becomes burning, all contiguous spaces also become burning. Otherwise, oiled terrain lasts indefinitely.

Caustic Terrain

Caustic terrain is covered in extremely corrosive liquid that eats away at anything immersed in it. When a character enters caustic terrain for the first time on a round or ends their turn there, they suffer one stress. A character kneeling or knocked-down in caustic terrain becomes corroding and suffers three stress instead of one, while a character completely submerged in caustic terrain suffers five stress instead. Caustic terrain normally loses its potency after one minute.

Blessed Terrain

Blessed terrain has been hallowed by a force of justice. When a character enters blessed terrain for the first time in a round or ends their turn there, they must lose -3 focus or they become blessed. Undead and fiends add a bane to attack actions against targets in blessed terrain. Blessed terrain removes but does not replace cursed terrain. Blessed terrain returns to normal after 1 minute.

Cursed Terrain

Cursed terrain has been desecrated by a force of evil. When a character enters cursed terrain for the first time in a round or ends their turn there, they must lose -3 focus or they become cursed. A bane is added to all attack actions against undead or fiends in cursed terrain. Cursed terrain removes but does not replace blessed terrain. Cursed terrain returns to normal after 1 minute.

Obscuring Cloud

An obscuring cloud fills the are above the terrain with dense mist or smoke. Characters are unable to see more than one space through an obscuring cloud. Abilities that pass through more than one space of obscuring terrain can’t be aimed properly and are ineffective, unless they are area abilities (but not line abilities). All visual actions suffer a bane against targets in an obscuring cloud, and characters in an obscuring cloud have their perception decreased by two. The obscuring cloud dissipates at the end of the round if it can flow downhill or if a strong wind is present, otherwise it lasts 1 minute. Depending on the source, an obscuring cloud may also exist underwater, at the GM’s discretion.

Toxic Cloud

A toxic cloud fills the air above the terrain with flammable, poisonous vapors. Characters who find themselves in the toxic cloud without first holding their breath on their turn gain the choking condition, which cannot be removed as long as they remain in the cloud. The toxic cloud is flammable and is replaced by burning terrain if it is in the area of a fire area or line ability or if is includes a burning character. The toxic cloud dissipates at the end of the round if it can flow downhill or if a strong wind is present, otherwise it lasts 1 minute.

Lava

Lava is extremely dangerous molten rock. When a character enters lava for the first time in a round or ends their turn there, they immediately become burning and suffer five stress. A character knocked-down, kneeling or immersed in lava suffers 5d6 stress instead. Many lesser foes will be defeated as soon as they are knocked into lava. If a space of lava overlaps with a space of frozen terrain or wet terrain, or if it is in the area of a ice or water area or line ability, then the lava solidifies into relatively safe stone. However, if the space is part of a larger body of lava, it will probably melt and return to lava at the end of the round.
