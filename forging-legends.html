Forging Legends

Legendary items are powerful relics with extraordinary effects and abilities. Yet these artifacts are not simply manufactured by famous smiths or enchanted by powerful wizards. Heroic and momentous deeds will be remembered as legends, and those legends themselves hold power. A legendary item is imbued with the spirit of the wonderous event it symbolizes.

Legendary Deeds

The first step toward forging a legend is achieving a legendary deed. A legendary deed is a feat that will be recounted and admired for countless generations to come.

Whenever the player characters do something extraordinary, the GM may declare that they have achieved a legendary deed. There are many ways to accomplish a legendary deed, such as defeating a powerful foe, ingeniously overcoming an obstacle, or performing an amazing feat of skill. There are no limits on what could be considered a legendary deed, but the deed must be truly legendary - a tale worth retelling through the ages!

Some of the more common way to achieve a legendary deed are to defeat a legendary foe, such as a mighty dragon, or to succeed on a talent roll of legendary difficulty.

Players should nominate the actions of other group members if their deeds seem suitably legendary. However, the GM may make the final decision. A legendary deed may not always be recognized right away. If the group comes to realize how incredible a past action truly was, the GM may declare the past action to be a legendary deed.

Affirming a Legend

Once a legendary deed has been achieved, it must then be affirmed. This is a process of discussion between the players and the GM detailing how the legend should be remembered and expressed in the game. There are four details each legend must have to be affirmed: a major effect, a minor effect, a symbol, and a name.

The players must decide the nature or essence of the legendary item’s effects, and the GM should decide the specific powers and limitations based on the players’ decisions. The effects of the item must be closely tied to legendary deed that creates them. Precisely how close may be determined by the GM, but the connection between the events of the legendary deed and the effects of the item must be clear and obvious to anyone who is told.

If one character stands out in the events of legendary deed, their opinion should be held in higher regard. But ultimately, affirming a legend is an activity to be shared by the whole group.

** **

Major Effects

Major effects are the most important part of legendary items, and the things for which such items will be renowned. Many major effects will take the form of powerful abilities. They may be combat abilities or be valuable for exploration and interaction. They may be passive abilities that are always in effect, abilities that must be recovered between uses, or powerful abilities that can only ever be used once.

The GM should decide on a suitable major effect based on the player’s suggestions. If the players aren’t satisfied with the GM’s decision, they may request different a major effect. But that too should be balanced by the GM. For balance guidelines, see the GM Guidelines chapter. Some examples of major effect include:

  • Attacks with the item may gain the fire tag.

  • The item may be used to cause plants in the area to grow rapidly and restrain foes.

  • The item may be used to reveal things that otherwise could not be seen.

  • The item grants immunity to some specific condition.

  • The item instantly returns to its owner’s hand when commanded.

Minor Effects

Minor effects have little to no direct benefit, but instead symbolize the spirit and meaning of the legend. Minor effect may include aesthetic effects, slight convinces, or demonstrations of the item’s power. Minor effects should never grant abilities. Some examples of minor effects include:

  • The item glows with harmless fire at the bearer’s command.

  • Flowers blossom in the footsteps of the bearer

  • The bearer’s eyes glow with power.

  • The item can never be damaged, tarnished, cracked, or ripped.

  • The owner can always sense their distance from the item.

Symbol & Name

The symbol of the legend is a physical object that will bear the power of the legendary deed such as a weapon, a piece of equipment, or some other item. Choosing an object closely tied to the events of the legendary deed may make the connection between deed and effects more obvious, allowing a broader range of effects. However, the symbol may also be unrelated to the events of the legendary deed.

Each object may only be the symbol for one legend at a time. One legend may be replaced by another on a specific symbol, but only if the two legends are related in a way that feels like one continuous story. For example, a wicked and evil blade may be redeemed by defeating the villain who forged it.

If the chosen symbol is a weapon or other piece of equipment with existing abilities, the legendary effects will probably replace one of the item’s abilities. Legendary items take on a life of their own and are not strictly upgrades to existing items.

The name of the legend may be anything that the players choose, but it should inspire memories of the people and events that created it.

** **

Inscribing a Legend

Finally, once the group has affirmed the legend, one character of the group’s choice may inscribe the legend. The chosen character must inscribe runes representing the name of the legend onto the chosen symbol over a few hours. Once the character has finished inscribing the runes, the legend has been forged, and the new legendary item is ready to use!

Legendary Quests

Many legendary deeds arise spontaneously out of heroic bravery, the demands of desperate situations, or just plain good luck. But some legendary items are created by grand aspirations and hard work. Legendary quests allow players to forge a specific legendary item of their choosing, but only after overcoming an appropriate adventure.

A player seeking to undertake a legendary quest should announce their intention to the GM. The player may describe any kind of legendary item they want, not merely one related to recent deeds. As with affirming a legend, the GM and the player should come to an agreement on the item’s powers, effects, and limitations.

As part of this discussion, the GM should determine a legendary quest needed to earn this item. The legendary quest could be anything from a single encounter to an epic odyssey, as befitting the legendary item. But whatever the quest, it must be symbolically related to the effects of the legendary item it will create.

If the player does not accept the legendary quest that the GM requires, or if the GM can’t offer a suitable legendary quest at the time, then nothing happens.

If the player does accept the legendary quest offered to them, then they should keep track of it and work toward it as best they can. Once legendary quest has been overcome, that completes the legendary deed needed to forge the chosen legend.

Each character may only undertake one legendary quest at a time. A character may abandon a legendary quest at any time, but giving up on one’s dreams is disheartening. A character that abandons a legendary quest after they have accepted it suffers three stress.

Legends of Old

Not all legendary items will be created by the players. Many legends have come and passed already, and those legacies remain. All legendary items are the symbols for legendary deeds of the past and have effects that reflect those epic histories.

Holding and meditating upon an unknown legendary item for one minute imparts a vague knowledge of the legendary deed that created it. However, the major and minor effects must be discovered independently, usually by trial and error. Once they have been discovered, the full history of the legendary item reveals itself to the person who has mastered it. Heroes are remembered, but legends never die.
