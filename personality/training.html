Training WIP

Your training describes your familiarity with certain tasks, and benefits your rolls related to those tasks. When you create your character, select 7 tasks from the list below in which your character is trained. When the GM calls for a roll, you should ask if your training would help with the task. If the GM agrees that it would help, add a +2 bonus to your TN for the roll. Some tasks may only be attempted by a character who has the necessary training.

Training Use
Run Run quickly or over long distances.
Jump Jump high and far.
Climb Climb up trees, rocks, buildings or other surfaces.
Swim Swim while laden and through turbulent water.
Heave Push, pull, lift, or haul heavy objects.
Smash Break through barriers and destroy tough objects.
Lurk Find a place to hide and stay there unnoticed.
Skulk Move while remaining out of sight, keeping to shadows and behind cover.
Tiptoe Move or interact with objects while remaining silent.
Tinker Fiddle with locks or other delicate mechanisms.
Balance Keep your footing, or land safely when you fall.
Contort Squeeze into or out of a tight spot, or flex into unusual positions.
Spot Notice subtle visual details.
Listen Pick up faint sounds or filter out noise.
Rummage Search through physical objects to find those of interest.
Inspect Search through information to find what stands out.
Medicine Heal or diagnose an ailment or injury.
Ride Control your mount or your position while mounted.
Slight of Hand Conceal your subtle hand movements.
Boast Convince others that you are important and deserve respect.
Intimidate Pressure others into giving you what you want.
Calm Defuse a tense situation and turn it into a conversation.
Disguise Look and act as if you were someone else.
Negotiate Help two sides come to a mutually agreeable arrangement.
Inspire Show others that you know what is best for all.
Perform Create and participate in musical or theatrical productions.
Schmooze Make a good first impression.
Divert change the topic away from one you’d rather avoid.
Manipulate Trick others into acting against their best interests.
Explain sincerely change a person’s belief.
Vow Convince others that you and your cause are sincere.
